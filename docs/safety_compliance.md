# Explosion & Laser Safety Compliance Checklist

## 1. Class 4 Laser Safety Systems (IEC 60825-1)
- [ ] **Interlocked Enclosure**: Mechanical switches instantly cut power to the 200W fiber laser if the chamber door is opened.
- [ ] **Laser Rated Windows**: Viewports must use certified 1070nm OD6+ blocking acrylic shielding.
- [ ] **Status Indicator Lights**: A physical warning light tower outside the lab indicates whether the laser is active (Red) or safe (Green).
- [ ] **PPE Availability**: All operators inside the room must wear calibrated 1064-1070nm OD7+ laser safety glasses.

## 2. Combustible Metal Dust Mitigation (NFPA 484)
- [ ] **Class D Fire Extinguisher**: A specialized Class D powder extinguisher is mounted within 10 feet of the printer. Never use water on burning metal.
- [ ] **Static Grounding**: The printing machine, powder storage containers, and the operator must be grounded to prevent static spark ignition.
- [ ] **Explosion-Proof Vacuum**: Material cleanup must only use an industrial, wet-mix immersion vacuum certified for hazardous metal dust.
- [ ] **Air Filtration**: Lab HVAC must utilize non-sparking fans and specialized particulate filters.

## 3. Atmospheric Interlocks & Gas Handling
- [ ] **Gas Purge Verification**: Hardware logic locks the laser trigger if oxygen levels exceed 0.1% inside the chamber.
- [ ] **Pressure Relief Valves**: Mechanically prevents over-pressurization of the sealed chamber during high-volume Argon flow.
- [ ] **Ambient O2 Monitor**: A wall-mounted room oxygen monitor alerts operators in case of an Argon gas leak, preventing asphyxiation hazards.
